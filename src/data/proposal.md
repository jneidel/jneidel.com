# Workshop proposals

## Format

I got multiple possible formats in mind:

### Format 1: One long workshop

**Pros**
- Get really into it, with a group of people

**Cons**
- Long/big time investment
- Topics that aren't of interest to all (reduces topic variety, common denominator)
- If people are too fast (and they aren't helping others) they might get bored (difference in experience)
- Might be overkill (too much info, too much to play around with at home)

### Format 2: Series of workshops, spread out over multiple days/weeks

**Pros**
- People can pick and choose only those workshop that are of interest to them
- Popular workshops can be easily repeated/scaled up
- Small time investment, more flexible

**Cons**
- You have to start over with every group (getting settled, explaining the basics)
- More orga

## Content

The staters and main courses can be mixed and matched depending on the
overarching topic of the workshop.

### Starters

Like lectures, I explain something, elaborate on questions. All short in length.

- Encryption (Why do we need it, how does it work (basics))
- Open Source (Why it is important for security and why you shouldn't use telegram)
- PKI (What is public key crypto)
- ? Threat actors (Who do we want to defend against)
- Survailance (Phone records, How the NSA works, what you can do about it)
- Google bad (google and facebooks business models, why they are not to be trusted)
- Disk Encryption (what it is, uses)

### Main Course

As an interactive workshop.
I showcase the program (use cases), explain what it does,
explain why this software might be picked for this job.
Followed by install instructions (either go through them shortly together, or
show them the whole process live), either way I'll have the process detailed on
some webpage as a reference point (wiki?).
At this point the group should be familiar with the workings of the program in
question and prepared to install it themselves. Which they will do at this
point.

Length (approx.) is specified in brackets.
